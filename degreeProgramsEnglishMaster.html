Master in Contemporary China Studies

Introduction

The Contemporary China Studies Program is an English-taught two-year master’s program that resides within our School, China’s premier institution of higher learning in politics, diplomacy, and international affairs.

The CCSP faculty includes over 20 professors from various departments and is assisted by a young and international administrative team.

The program recruits students from all over the world, fostering a unique multidisciplinary and multinational environment in which diverse backgrounds and experiences enhance rigorous academic study.

Program Structure

During the first year, CCSP students explore issues related to the contemporary Chinese economy, politics, law, society, and foreign policy. A wide range of elective courses can also be selected by other Renmin University Schools. Besides in-class learning, the program organizes field trips to relevant institutions and organizations. These typically include think tanks, private and state-owned enterprises, SEZs, rural areas, as well as cultural and historical sites.

Most students finish their coursework within the first year and dedicate their second year to internships and thesis writing. The thesis is an integral part of the Contemporary China Studies Program. Students select a supervisor at the end of their first year and work closely with their advisor to produce valuable research.

Fees and Scholarships

Tuition for the Master of Contemporary China Studies Program is RMB 60,000 per academic year for the regular study period of two years. Yearly tuition is due during registration at the beginning of each academic year. All applicants are eligible for a number of scholarships, including the Chinese Government Scholarship and the tuition refund offered by Renmin University.

For more information about applications, courses, tuition fees, and scholarships, please consult the CCSP website or reach out to the CCSP Team at chinastudies@ruc.edu.cn.
